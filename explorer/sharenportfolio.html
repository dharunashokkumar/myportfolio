Background

About

I believe great enterprise software should feel effortless, even when solving complex problems. My approach combines systematic thinking with deep user empathy to create products that scale without sacrificing soul.

Before tech, I spent three years as an architect at a leading design firm, creating spaces for human interaction. This background shapes how I think about digital wayfinding and information architecture—every click should feel as intuitive as walking through a thoughtfully designed building.

At my current role, I lead design for observability tools processing billions of events daily. We're constantly exploring the edge of data density versus clarity, helping engineers spot critical patterns in oceans of metrics. It's like designing a telescope that can show both galaxies and atoms.

I mentor designers through ADPList, contribute to open-source design systems, and speak about designing for technical audiences. Currently fascinated by how spatial computing will reshape enterprise tools and the role of AI in augmenting design decisions.
