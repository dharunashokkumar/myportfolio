About Dharun Ashokkumar

Dharun Ashokkumar is a software developer and open source contributor from India. He is currently pursuing a Bachelor of Technology (BTech) in Computer Science Engineering at Lovely Professional University in Punjab, India. His work spans web development, Internet of Things (IoT) systems, cybersecurity research, self-hosted infrastructure, and cloud computing. He actively contributes to open source projects through his GitHub profile and believes in building in public.

Dharun's technical interests are broad and applied. He works primarily with Python, JavaScript, React, Node.js, Docker, and Linux, and has hands-on experience with hardware platforms including Arduino, ESP32, and Raspberry Pi. His projects range from IoT vending machines with QR code payment systems to desktop AI assistants, distributed web applications, and penetration testing research. He approaches technology as a means to solve real problems rather than as an end in itself.

Early Life and Technical Journey

Dharun Ashokkumar began his technical journey with no prior programming background or electronics experience. His introduction to technology came through the Arduino Uno, which he used for his earliest experiments in electronics and embedded programming. The experience of making an LED blink for the first time proved to be a formative moment, sparking a curiosity that would drive years of self-directed learning.

From those initial experiments, Dharun progressed to increasingly complex Arduino projects: connecting LCD displays for custom messages, integrating RFID modules for card reading, working with seven-segment displays, and building sensor-based systems for temperature measurement and motion detection. Each project built his understanding of circuits, microcontroller programming, and systematic problem-solving.

The discovery of the Raspberry Pi marked a significant turning point. Dharun transformed a Raspberry Pi 4 into a home cloud server, learning Linux system administration, DDNS configuration, Cloudflare tunnels for remote access, and network security fundamentals in the process. The Raspberry Pi bridged his understanding of hardware with real-world computing and server infrastructure.

The ESP32 development board expanded his capabilities further into wireless and IoT territory. With its built-in Wi-Fi and Bluetooth, the ESP32 enabled Dharun to build systems that communicated wirelessly, streamed video, scanned QR codes, and controlled devices remotely. One of his most notable early projects was a QR code-based vending machine prototype that integrated Firebase, web technologies, and hardware control over Wi-Fi.

Throughout this period, curiosity remained the primary driver. Dharun taught himself each new technology by building real projects rather than following tutorials alone. He embraced failure as a core part of the learning process, having fried components, crashed servers, and written countless bugs before arriving at working solutions.
