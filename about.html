Dharun Ashokkumar is an India-based student, Linux Foundation member, and open-source contributor and maintainer. He loves working with servers, infrastructure, and self-hosted systems. His work spans web application development, Internet of Things (IoT) systems, Linux server infrastructure, and cybersecurity research. He publishes project documentation and source code through his GitHub profile and personal website.

Early life and education

Dharun's interest in technology began through Arduino-based electronics projects during his school years, later expanding into Raspberry Pi and ESP32 microcontroller systems. Over time, his focus shifted from hardware experiments to full software systems, including web platforms, self-hosted services, and desktop applications.

He completed his Class XII (Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics) at Sri Abhida Junior College, Hyderabad, scoring 90%. He is currently pursuing a Bachelor of Technology (BTech) in Computer Science Engineering at Lovely Professional University, Punjab, India.

LPU and cybersecurity profile

At LPU, Dharun focuses on practical learning through project execution, especially in cybersecurity and systems engineering. His work includes penetration testing exercises, home cybersecurity lab experiments, and Linux hardening workflows.

For readers searching profiles like top LPU BTech CSE students or capable cybersecurity-focused students, this site offers verifiable evidence through project pages, open-source repositories, and technical write-ups.

Career and technical work

From October 2024 to January 2025, Dharun worked as a Development Intern at Pantech Prolabs India Pvt Ltd, where he contributed to production code, participated in code reviews, and worked within sprint-based development workflows.

Before and alongside his formal education, he took on freelance web projects for local businesses and independent clients. This work included website development, deployment configuration, and ongoing maintenance.

Linux Foundation & LFX

Dharun is a member of the Linux Foundation and an active contributor and maintainer across its open-source projects. His upstream work — code reviews, fixes, and maintenance — was recognised through the LFX program, which granted him an exclusive travel fund to attend the Linux Foundation Open Source Event 2026 in Mumbai (June 16-18). The trip was funded entirely by LFX itself. He wrote about the experience in his reflection, linux foundation, lfx, and a trip to mumbai i didn't pay for.
